To-Be: the path inside Brio

The same person, the same job, now inside Brio. The honesty rule flips: the future has to be designed, so instead of "do not invent," every step and feature must trace to a real As-Is barrier or a job. A feature with no parent pain is cut.

Focus

Priya, the Handed-It Operator (primary persona) x her main job.

"When I am handed responsibility for employee wellbeing with no HR training, I want a guided system that tells me what to run and when, so that I feel competent and the team actually benefits."

Source: user-research/docs/personas.md, user-research/docs/jtbd.md (Main Job).

Audit

Critique and Risks

A consolidated review across cjm-as-is.md and cjm-to-be.md: orphans, discrepancies with the MVP core, and the dangerous [?] that survive.

Danger questions

1
Kill-condition. The privacy bundle (item 4) is MVP and its success signal is H2 (participation 65%), the riskiest assumption and still unproven. The doresearch nuance shows participation is multi-driver, so a RAND-adjacent 40% is a more defensible floor. Open: does the bundle move SMB participation to 65%, and if closer to 40%, does a ~15-person team reliably clear minimum-N=5?
Status: [?], closed by the Lean UX Canvas First Test (no-product prototype, kill below 40%), not by this map.
2
MVP padding. Threshold alert (7) and weekly digest (9) are important but do not break the To-Be path. Reclassified MVP-supporting to avoid over-building before H2 is validated.
3
Tracing precision. The bundle (item 4) traces to employee voice ("harvest and sell," "traceable"), but the operator is the buyer. It drives participation (H2), not activation (H1), so it must be shown to the operator as proof, not only to the employee.
