Owner dashboard

The owner's surface: one honest, aggregate-only read of team wellbeing in under two minutes. One number, whether it is up or down, the plain-language meaning, and a clear statement of what the owner can and cannot see. It is the paid feature unlocked in cluster 7, and it is a read, not a tool: no navigation tabs, by design. For a different persona (Marcus the owner). It hosts the 9.1 privacy section and the 9.3 empty state. Private surface, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/owner-dashboard.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up. The whole read fits a short scroll; the owner often checks on a phone.

  1. Minimal owner header: the Brio mark and the company name, with an "Owner view" label. No job tabs (deliberate contrast to the operator app).
  2. The pulse read (hero): a single plain-language level and one honest sentence. Never a vanity metric (Principle 4).
  3. Trend: a simple trend over the last several cycles, so the direction is clear at a glance.
  4. Participation: as a share of the team, never who. Tells the owner the signal is real without exposing anyone.
  5. 9.1 "What I can and cannot see": the 0.3 owner variant, two plain lists. Can see the aggregate; cannot see individuals, names, who answered, or any drill-down (E2, item 4).
  6. Quiet footer note: when the data updates, that the read is under two minutes, and a link to the latest digest (9.2).

Components used: a minimal owner header (distinct chrome, not the operator 0.1 nav), the pulse element (from 4.0 / 4.1 / 7.0), and the 0.3 owner-variant privacy section (9.1). No new global; 9.1 is a 0.3 variant.

Private owner surface

SEO block (noindex)

Owner dashboard is an authenticated, private surface. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The in-app findability rules still apply.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (private owner zone)
canonicalnone for search; surface URL is /owner
schema.orgnone
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the pulse reading or the page title) for the accessible document outline
  • the number and reading are text, not an image; participation is stated as a share, never as who
  • the can-see / cannot-see boundary is conveyed in text and labels, not by color alone
  • the empty state states the minimum-N reason in text; tap targets at least 44px

One number, one honest sentence, one privacy statement, no navigation, under two minutes. It is where J5 (prove value) and E2 (owner comfort) are paid off, and where the aggregate-only promise is restated on the owner's own screen.
