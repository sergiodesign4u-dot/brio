Upgrade / paywall

Take the payment that unlocks the owner dashboard, in a focused modal over 7.1. The operator confirms the tier, sees the seats and the price from team size, enters payment, and upgrades. Because it is a paywall, "Stay on Free" is always present, so the operator is never trapped and never charged by surprise. A pure 0.5 Dialog instance; item 8 and the business model. Inside the operator app, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/upgrade.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first thing in the dialog. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up. The recoverable exit is always visible, never hidden.

  1. Dialog title and close: "Upgrade to Starter" with a close control. Close equals Stay on Free: a no-charge exit.
  2. Tier: the Starter tier and the line that matters here, the owner dashboard is included (0.6 tiers).
  3. Seats: prefilled from the current team size, with the per-seat price. Per-seat pricing, so seats drive the total.
  4. Price: the computed monthly total (seats times per-seat), billed monthly. Pricing is a hypothesis, benchmarked.
  5. Payment: a card block (card number, expiry, security code). The structural place for the payment-provider element.
  6. Primary and recoverable exit: "Upgrade now" (pay) as the primary; "Stay on Free" as the always-present exit back to 7.0. On failure, to 7.3.

Components used: the canonical 0.5 Dialog / modal shell (scrim, centered modal on desktop, bottom sheet on mobile, full a11y contract), the 0.6 Starter tier, and standard payment inputs. No new global; this is a 0.5 instance, registered as a 0.5 consumer.

Payment, recovery, and exit

States and primary CTA

Honest paywall: the price is shown before payment, the exit is never hidden, and a failure recovers to 7.3 rather than trapping.

Operator app modal

SEO block (noindex)

Upgrade or paywall is a modal inside the authenticated operator app. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The in-app findability and dialog a11y rules still apply.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (no standalone URL; a modal over 7.1)
schema.orgnone
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • the dialog has an accessible name (the title) and the 0.5 a11y contract (role, aria-modal, focus trap, Esc, inert background, focus return)
  • the price and any error are conveyed in text, not by color alone
  • the payment inputs are labelled; Upgrade now and Stay on Free are labelled controls
  • the exit is reachable by keyboard (Esc) and pointer; tap targets at least 44px
An honest paywall. Upgrade or paywall is a pure 0.5 Dialog instance (registered as a 0.5 consumer) and reuses the 0.6 Starter tier; it invents nothing global. The price is shown before payment, the exit is always present, and a failure recovers to 7.3 rather than trapping. Next: 7.3 Payment failed (the recoverable error state), which closes cluster 7.
