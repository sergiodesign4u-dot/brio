Invite owner + upgrade

Turn the intent from 7.0 into a real invite. The operator enters the owner's email, sees plainly that the owner dashboard is a paid feature, and either upgrades (free plan) or sends the invite directly (paid plan). The privacy boundary was set on 7.0, so here the reminder is condensed and the focus is the email, the plan, and the unlock. Serves S2 and item 8 (the paid unlock). Inside the operator app, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/invite-owner.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up. The paywall is never a surprise: 7.0 and the plan comparison both state the owner dashboard is paid before the action.

  1. 0.1 Navigation: the operator app nav with the Owner tab active.
  2. Heading: "Invite your owner".
  3. Owner email field: a labelled email field (and an optional owner name), so the invite reaches the right person.
  4. Plan comparison: a compact free-versus-Starter comparison with the owner dashboard as the paid unlock (0.6 tiers). Pricing is a hypothesis, benchmarked.
  5. Condensed privacy reminder: one 0.3 line ("your owner sees aggregate only, never individual data"), continuity with 7.0.
  6. Primary action (plan-dependent): free = "Upgrade and invite" (opens 7.2); paid = "Send invite". On success, the "invite sent" confirmation.

Components used: 0.1 (Owner tab), the 0.6 plan tiers, a condensed 0.3 reminder, the 7.2 Upgrade dialog (opened from the primary on the free plan), and the 2.2 confirmation layout (the "invite sent" success). No new global.

SEO block (noindex)

Invite owner and upgrade is inside the authenticated operator app. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The in-app findability rules still apply.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (deep app zone, like Dashboard, Team, and Share)

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the heading) for the accessible document outline
  • the email field is a labelled input; the plan comparison is a real list, not an image, and prices are text
  • the primary control states when it leads to an upgrade
  • the confirmation is conveyed in text and a mark, not by color alone; tap targets at least 44px
The business model, in the product. Invite owner and upgrade reuses 0.1, the 0.6 plan tiers, a condensed 0.3 reminder, the 7.2 Upgrade dialog, and the 2.2 confirmation layout; it invents nothing global. It is the paid unlock of flow F3: the operator upgrades to give the owner an aggregate-only dashboard, with the paywall never a surprise. Next: 7.2 Upgrade or paywall (the dialog, a 0.5 instance), then 7.3 Payment failed (recoverable, not a trap), which closes cluster 7.
