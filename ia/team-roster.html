Team roster

The operator invites the team, sees who is in, and reads how much of the team takes part, all without ever learning who answered. The roster knows who is on the team because the operator invited them; it is never linked to who answered a check-in. Serves the main job and inviting the team. Inside the operator app, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/team-roster.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up.

  1. Nav + header (0.1 operator, Team active): H1 "Your team", subhead with team size.
  2. Invite block: the join link with a copy control, the join code, and an email-invite field. Same mechanism as 3.2, standalone here.
  3. Team size + participation: invited and joined counts, and the participation rate as a share ("72% answered"), never who; follows the D1 no-live-count rule.
  4. Members list: invited members with name or email and a status (invited or joined), each with resend or manage (to 6.1). Invite identity only, never connected to answers.
  5. Privacy boundary callout: the wall between roster identity and answer identity (see below).
  6. App shell footer (minimal): privacy link (1.1) and help.

Components used: 0.1 Navigation, 0.3 Privacy-disclosure (condensed), 0.5 Dialog (via the 6.1 manage dialog). The invite block and members list are thin in-node elements. No new global invented.

The make-or-break boundary

Roster identity is not answer identity

Two kinds of "who" exist on this page, and only one is ever visible.

Kind of "who"Visible?What it is
Roster identityYeswho is on the team; the operator invited them, so name or email and an invited/joined status show
Answer identityNeverwho answered a check-in, and what they said; never shown, never linked to the roster, cannot be derived

The members list and the participation rate sit on the same page but are never joined. The operator can see that a person is on the team and that 72% of the team answered, but never that that person answered, or what they said.

Private app zone

SEO block (noindex)

Team roster is inside the operator app, an authenticated per-account view. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The in-app findability rules still apply.

robotsnoindex, nofollow
canonicalnone for search; app URL is /app/team
schema.orgnone (not a public landing page)
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 ("Your team") for the accessible document outline
  • the join code and link are selectable text with labeled copy controls
  • the members list is a real list with a status per row in text, not color-only
  • the participation share has a text label; tap targets at least 44px
Reuse only, wall intact. Team roster reuses 0.1, 0.3, and 0.5 (via 6.1) and invents nothing global; the invite block and members list are thin in-node elements. It respects the D1 no-live-count rule and keeps roster identity and answer identity strictly apart. Next: 6.1 Resend or manage invite (the small 0.5 dialog), then cluster 3, the onboarding wizard.
