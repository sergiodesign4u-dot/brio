Home

The front door and the return door, and the exemplar for every page that follows. It leads the primary persona to her main job in one clear step and gives a returning operator a fast way back in. Serves Main and item 1 (self-serve start). Indexed. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/home.md.

The planks

Per-page IA template

Every page node follows this order. Home is the first page built to it; the rest follow at Step 5.

  1. Page name, type, node number, surface
  2. Purpose (one line) and the job(s) it serves
  3. URL / slug and breadcrumbs
  4. Content blocks, mobile-first priority (block 1 is the first screen)
  5. Components used (from the library) and variants
  6. States (guest / logged-in / role / empty / loading / error)
  7. Filters or facets (listing pages only)
  8. Primary CTA (one clear next step)
  9. Adaptive notes (mobile stack, then desktop)
  10. SEO block A to E (indexed) or a noindex note (private)
Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up.

  1. Nav + hero (0.1 guest/logged-in): H1, one-line subhead, primary CTA, secondary link to how privacy works (1.1).
  2. State strip: CTA and greeting change by auth state (Start free vs Go to your dashboard).
  3. How it works (3 steps): pick a program, invite the team, read the team pulse (mirrors F1).
  4. Privacy proof (0.3 public variant): "Aggregate-only. We never show individual responses," link to 1.1.
  5. Programs preview: the four categories (0.6), linking to the library or a 1.5 landing.
  6. Pricing teaser: free to start, owner dashboard is paid, link to 1.0.
  7. FAQ teaser: privacy, setup, billing, link to 1.4.
  8. Final CTA band: Start free.
  9. Footer (0.2 full).
Social proof (logos, testimonials) is ?, not invented; a placeholder block is reserved and left empty until there is real proof.

Components used: 0.1 Navigation (guest / logged-in), 0.3 Privacy-disclosure (public trust-strip variant), 0.4 Program card (optional preview), 0.2 Footer, 0.6 Canonical lists.

C. Ready SEO text

Brio is employee wellbeing software for small companies that have no HR team. Whoever got handed people work, an office manager, an ops lead, or the founder, gets a guided way to run real wellbeing programs. The owner gets honest proof it is working at the team level. Individual answers are never shown to anyone, because the privacy boundary is the product, not a setting you can switch off.
