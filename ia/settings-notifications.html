Notifications

Where the operator decides what Brio sends and how often: the check-in reminders that keep participation up, the weekly team digest, the monthly owner digest (9.2), and how the threshold alert (4.2) is delivered. Unlike the read-only 8.2 Privacy center, these are real toggles. Email is the MVP channel; Slack is a later phase. Notifications never include individual data. For Priya the operator. Serves J4, item 9, item 10. Authenticated app view, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/settings-notifications.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Each notification is a toggle row: a label, a description, and an on or off switch, with cadence where it matters. The reminder cadence is the most consequential control, because it drives participation (H2).

  1. Check-in reminders: a toggle plus a cadence (weekly by default, optional send day). The gentle nudge that keeps participation up (J4).
  2. Weekly team digest (to you): a toggle for the operator's weekly summary (item 9).
  3. Monthly owner digest: a toggle for the owner's monthly email (item 10), the same digest as 9.2; disabled with a reason on a free plan or if no owner is invited.
  4. Threshold alert delivery: a toggle for how the early-warning alert (4.2) reaches the operator, so a meaningful drop is not missed.
  5. Channel and privacy note: email in the MVP, Slack deferred; a note that notifications never include individual data.

Components used: the 8.0 shell chrome, a toggle-row pattern (label, description, and an on or off switch whose state is shown by position and text, not color), and cadence selects. The digests and alert this controls are 9.2 and 4.2. No new global is invented.

Black and white, wireframe level

Wireframe

Primary CTA: none single; the toggles and cadence selects are the controls, and changes save in place. The reminder cadence (participation, H2) and the owner digest (item 10) are the most consequential.
