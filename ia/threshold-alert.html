Threshold alert detail

When the aggregate drops meaningfully, this tells the operator plainly what changed, what it means, and the one thing to do next, so a struggle is caught early (J2). It is honest about a real dip yet calm, and strictly team-level, never pointing at a person. Serves J2 and E1. Inside the operator app, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/threshold-alert.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up.

  1. Nav + header (0.1 operator, Dashboard active): H1 "What changed". Back to 4.0.
  2. What dropped: the specific aggregate move, plainly, "from Steady to Low over the last two cycles," with a small down-trend indicator. Aggregate only, never who.
  3. Plain-language reading (E1): what it means, calm, no jargon; a team-level dip, not a person, and explicitly not a diagnosis of anyone.
  4. Suggested next action: one concrete step, the primary CTA, usually See the program (to 5.1) or Review the full pulse (to 4.1).
  5. Link to the relevant program: a compact reference to the program tied to this signal, to 5.1.
  6. Privacy reassurance (0.3 condensed): a team-level signal that never names an individual, to 1.1.
  7. App shell footer (minimal): privacy link (1.1) and help.

Components used: 0.1 Navigation, 0.3 Privacy-disclosure (condensed). The what-dropped indicator and reading reuse the in-node pulse elements from 4.0 and 4.1. No new global invented.

Two principles meet here

Honest, calm, and never individual

Principle 4 (honest signals over flattering ones) means the alert says plainly the team dipped, it does not soften a real decline. The privacy boundary means it stays strictly aggregate, reporting a team-level move and pointing at a program to act on, never at a person, and saying so. The reading is calm and action-oriented, so the operator acts rather than panics or goes looking for who.

Private app zone

SEO block (noindex)

Threshold alert detail is inside the operator app, an authenticated per-account view. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The in-app findability rules still apply.

robotsnoindex, nofollow
canonicalnone for search; app URL is /app/alerts/:id
schema.orgnone (not a public landing page)
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 ("What changed") for the accessible document outline
  • the what-dropped move is stated in text (from, to, and the period), not by color or an icon alone
  • the next action is a labeled control; the program link carries its accessible name
  • tap targets at least 44px
