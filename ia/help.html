Help / FAQ

The main self-serve support hub and a key SEO surface: three FAQ groups (setting up, privacy, billing) in the 1.1 FAQ pattern, an in-help search, and a contact fallback to 1.3, so most questions resolve without contacting support and no user is a dead-end. The FAQ questions and answers are the FAQPage schema source and must match the visible text exactly; the privacy answers reuse the 1.1 canonical spine and must not drift. For Priya the operator. Serves supporting self-serve support and deflection. Indexed, with a full A to E SEO block. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/help.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Search first, then grouped answers, then a fallback: the page's job is deflection, so the strongest elements are the FAQ groups, not the CTA. The privacy group says the same thing the public explainer (1.1) and the pre-check-in disclosure (10.1) say.

  1. Public nav (0.1 pre-auth): the Brio mark, marketing links, Log in, Start free. No job tabs.
  2. Hero: H1 "How can we help?", a subhead. The LCP is the heading text.
  3. Search within help: a search box that filters the FAQ. Help-scoped, not the app-wide global search (11.5), which is likely N/A [?]; the FAQ text is in the HTML, not loaded only on search, so it stays crawlable.
  4. FAQ groups by topic: Setting up Brio, Privacy and your data, Plans and billing, each a set of question and answer rows (the 1.1 wf-faq pattern). These are the FAQPage schema source and must match the visible text exactly.
  5. Contact fallback: "still stuck? contact us", a link to 1.3, so a user who did not find an answer is never a dead-end.
  6. CTA band: Start free, to 2.0 Sign up. Footer (0.2).

Components used: 0.1 Navigation (public), 0.2 Footer, the public chrome from 1.0 and 1.1, the wf-faq groups from 1.1, and the wf-help fallback from 1.3. The in-help search is thin, page-specific layout; it ties to 11.5 Global search, which is likely N/A. No new global; the privacy answers must not drift from the 1.1 spine.

Black and white, wireframe level

Wireframe

No color and no brand: structure only, color arrives at Concept (stage 06). Desktop shows the full help hub; mobile stacks the same blocks. The FAQ answers are plain crawlable text, never carried by color; every answer stays visible for the crawler.

Setting up Brio
How do I get started with Brio?
Sign up, tell us your company size, and Brio pre-selects a first program for you; setup takes about five minutes.
How do I invite my team?
From the Team tab, share an invite link or a join code; people can take their first check-in without creating an account.
How long does a check-in take?
Under 30 seconds, one to three quick questions, with no account needed the first time.
Privacy and your data (same as 1.1)
Can my manager see my answer?
No. Answers are only ever shown as a team aggregate of at least 5 people.
What if my team is small?
If fewer than 5 answer, no results are shown at all, so no one can be singled out.
Do you sell my data?
No. Individual data is never shown or sold; the subscription pays for the aggregate.
Plans and billing
Is there a free plan?
Yes. The free plan covers up to 10 people with one active program and a basic weekly check-in.
What do I get when I upgrade?
Paid plans add the full program library, trends, and the owner dashboard; pricing is per seat.
Can I change or cancel anytime?
Yes, from Settings then Plan; changes take effect from the next billing cycle.

C. Ready SEO text

The Brio help center answers the questions operators ask most: how to set up your first program and invite your team, how the aggregate-only privacy model protects individual answers, and how plans and billing work. Search for a topic, or browse the groups below. If you are still stuck, contact support and we will reply to your email, usually within one business day.
