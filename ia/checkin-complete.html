Check-in complete / thank-you

The terminal screen of the employee check-in: confirm the answer landed, reassure that it joined the team's aggregate and is never seen on its own, and end calmly with no account push and nothing more to do. It closes the trust loop that 10.1 opened. No individual score is ever shown to the employee. For Yemi the employee. Serves J4 and E2; ends flow F2. Private token-gated surface, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/checkin-complete.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

A calm ending, centered and short. The persistent anonymity anchor is carried to the last screen so the promise is the last thing as well as the first (Principle 3).

  1. Minimal top bar with the anonymity anchor: the Brio mark, the company name, and the small "Anonymous" reminder.
  2. Confirmation: a check badge and a plain "Thanks, you're done" heading. Closure that the short task is complete (J4).
  3. Reassurance: one line that the answer joined the team's aggregate and is not seen on its own (ties back to the 10.1 promise). This is where E2 is honored at the end.
  4. No individual score, by design: the employee is not shown a personal score. Results are team-level only. (An employee-facing aggregate result, HJ2, is [?] parked.)
  5. Calm end: a quiet "you can close this" hint; no account prompt, no upsell. An optional "how your privacy is protected" link to the public 1.1 page is the only outbound path.

Components used: the 10.0 to 10.2 check-in chrome (no nav, no account) and a simple confirmation badge (the 2.2 confirmation idea, redrawn). No new global; the privacy content is not repeated (that is 10.1), only the short aggregate reassurance and the persistent anchor remain.

Black and white, wireframe level

Wireframe

No color and no brand: structure only, color arrives at Concept (stage 06). A short centered confirmation on a phone, with the aggregate reassurance and a calm end. There is no forward button, by design: the task is done and the natural end is to close the tab. The desktop version centers the same narrow card.

Private token-gated surface

SEO block (noindex)

Check-in complete is a private, token-gated employee surface. It is not indexed and carries no schema. It is a transactional zone, per the platform SEO stance.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (token-gated, personal)
canonicalnone for search; flow URL is /c/{invite-token}
schema.orgnone
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the thank-you heading) for the accessible document outline
  • the confirmation is conveyed by text and a mark, never by color alone
  • the optional privacy link is a labelled control; tap targets at least 44px
  • the flow works without an account
Closing the trust loop. Check-in complete ends flow F2 the way 10.1 opened it: the answer landed, it joined the aggregate, and it is never seen on its own, said plainly one last time. It reuses the 10.0 to 10.2 chrome and a confirmation badge, invents nothing global, and shows no individual score, by design. This completes the employee happy path (10.0 entry, 10.1 disclosure, 10.2 questions, 10.3 complete). Next: the recovery states 10.4 Invite link expired, 10.5 Already submitted, and 10.6 Submit error, which keep the employee flow from ever dead-ending.
