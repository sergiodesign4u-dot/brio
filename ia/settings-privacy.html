Privacy center

The operator-facing proof surface: exactly what you and the owner can and cannot see, built into how Brio works rather than a setting anyone can change. It renders the canonical 0.3 privacy-disclosure in its operator variant, the differentiator made checkable inside the product so the operator can stand behind it. The operator twin of 9.1 (owner) and 10.1 (employee): the same five claims, reframed. For Priya the operator. Serves J3, E2, item 4. Authenticated app view, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/settings-privacy.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Privacy is proof, not a control panel. The whole point is that nothing here is a setting; it is read-only, and it says the same thing the team and the public are told.

  1. Section context: inside the app chrome, "Settings / Privacy center"; on mobile a back to the Settings index.
  2. Intro and structural note: this is exactly what can and cannot be seen, and it is built in, not a setting, marked with a "Structural" lock label (Principle 1, founder decision D1).
  3. The five claims (operator framing): the canonical 0.3 spine, each an icon and one plain sentence, framed as "what you see".
  4. Can and cannot panel: can see the team score and trend and participation as a share; cannot see any individual, name, who answered, any drill-down, or any sub-group below 5.
  5. Same promise everywhere (quiet): a note that this is the same promise the team sees before answering (10.1) and the public explainer (1.1).

Components used: the canonical 0.3 privacy-disclosure (operator variant); the can and cannot panel reuses the wf-see and wf-seerow list from 7.0 and 9.0 (plus and x marks, never color); the claim list is the same five-claim spine as 10.1 and 9.1. Runs inside the 8.0 shell. No new global; 8.2 is a registered 0.3 instance that must not drift.

The differentiator, made checkable. This is the strategic dimension we must win (privacy visible in the UI, not promised in a policy) put where the operator can verify it and answer a team member who asks "who sees this?". It is deliberately read-only: nothing here is a toggle, because the boundary is structural (Principle 1, D1). The same five claims render for the employee (10.1), the owner (9.1), and the public (1.1); 0.3 is the single source and must not drift.

Authenticated app view

SEO block (noindex)

Privacy center is inside the operator app, an authenticated per-account view. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The indexable version of the same five claims lives on the public 1.1 Security and privacy page; the two must not drift (0.3 is the single source).

robotsnoindex, nofollow (authenticated app)
canonicalnone for search; app URL is /app/settings/privacy
schema.orgnone (public 1.1 carries the indexable version)
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 ("Privacy center" within the settings outline) for the accessible document outline
  • the claims and the can and cannot lists are text with plus and x marks, never carried by color alone
  • the structural note is text; the optional external links are labelled controls
  • tap targets at least 44px
Proof, not a control. Privacy center renders the canonical 0.3 five claims and the can and cannot panel, framed for the operator and marked structural, inside the 8.0 shell. It reuses the 0.3 component, the wf-see and wf-seerow list from 7.0 and 9.0, and the shell chrome, and invents nothing global; it is the operator twin of 9.1 and 10.1, the same promise reframed.
