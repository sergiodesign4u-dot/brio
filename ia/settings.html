Settings shell

The operator utility shell: one predictable place for account and configuration, as a container whose section list mirrors the four settings areas (Plan, Privacy center, Notifications, Account). It is a utility reached from a settings entry in the app chrome, not one of the four job tabs, so it never competes with the main-job navigation. On desktop the selected section renders in a pane beside the list; on mobile each row drills into its section. For Priya the operator. Supporting node; opens cluster 8. Authenticated app view, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/settings.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

The shell's whole job is the section list, the map of what can be configured. Settings sits beside the four job tabs as a utility, not as a tab, so the main job stays uncluttered (Principle 2).

  1. Nav + header (0.1 operator app chrome): the Brio mark and the four job tabs, none active, plus the Settings utility marked active. H1 "Settings".
  2. Section list (mirrors the areas): Plan (tier, seats, invoices, to 8.1), Privacy center (what the owner can and cannot see, to 8.2, the proof surface), Notifications (reminders and digests, to 8.3), Account (name, email, password, sign out, to 8.4).
  3. Account context (quiet): a small chip showing who is signed in; the full account controls live in 8.4.
  4. Section pane (desktop) or drill-in (mobile): on desktop the selected section renders in a pane beside the list (Plan by default); on mobile each row opens its section.

Components used: 0.1 Navigation (operator app chrome, the same wf-appbar and job tabs from 7.0, with Settings active rather than a job tab). The section list and pane are shell-specific layout, thin, not globals; the sections (8.1 to 8.4) render their own components, including the 0.3 privacy-disclosure inside 8.2. No new global is invented.

Authenticated app view

SEO block (noindex)

Settings is inside the operator app, an authenticated per-account view. It is not indexed and carries no schema.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (authenticated app)

hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 ("Settings") for the accessible document outline
  • the section list is a labelled navigation list; the active section is conveyed by text and state, not color alone
  • the account chip is text; Settings is reachable from the app chrome as a labelled utility control
  • tap targets at least 44px
