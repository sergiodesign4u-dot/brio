Sign up

The self-serve front door. An operator arriving from the Landing, Pricing, or a segment page creates a Brio account in seconds, with no credit card, and goes straight into onboarding. A focused conversion surface: minimal chrome, one clear action, one honest trust cue. Serves item 1 (self-serve start) and Main; it starts the main flow F1. Public but transactional, so the form is noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/signup.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up. This is a conversion page, so the chrome is deliberately minimal: a pre-auth header, not the four job tabs (those are post-auth).

  1. Pre-auth header: the Brio mark and a single "Log in" link (a reduced 0.1 variant, no job tabs).
  2. Heading and value line: "Create your Brio account" plus one benefit line ("Start free. No credit card."). Reinforces item 1.
  3. Sign-up form: a labelled email field and a labelled password field, primary CTA "Create account". The shortest credible form.
  4. Magic-link option: "Email me a link instead" (passwordless), to 2.2 Magic-link sent. Equal-weight, since passwordless suits a non-technical operator.
  5. Legal microcopy: "By creating an account you agree to our Terms and Privacy Policy", linking to 1.6.
  6. Privacy trust cue: one condensed 0.3 line ("team-level, aggregate signals, never individual data") where it first matters.
  7. Log-in link: "Already have an account? Log in", to 2.1.
  8. Footer: 0.2 footer, public variant (condensed).

Components used: a reduced 0.1 (pre-auth header), 0.2 (public footer), 0.3 (condensed trust line), links to 1.6. Standard form inputs, no new global. SSO (Google, Microsoft) is a paid feature, so it is deliberately absent from self-serve sign-up.

Public funnel, transactional form

SEO block (noindex, follow)

Sign up sits in the public funnel, but the form itself is not indexed. This is a deliberate decision, and it differs from the deep app zones (which are noindex, nofollow): here follow is kept so equity flows to the legal pages and Log in.

robotsnoindex, follow (thin, transactional; duplicated in intent by the marketing pages)
acquisition SEOlives on the indexed pages that funnel here: 1.0 Landing, 1.x Pricing, 1.5 segment / category landings, each with its own H1, schema, and target queries
branded queries"Brio sign up" is captured by Landing / Home, which links to this form
schema.orgnone on the form
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the heading) for the accessible document outline
  • email and password are labelled inputs with visible labels
  • errors are conveyed in text, not by color alone
  • the primary and secondary actions are labelled buttons; tap targets at least 44px
Flow F1 starts here. Sign up reuses a reduced 0.1 (pre-auth header), 0.2 (public footer), and a condensed 0.3 trust line, and links to 1.6; it invents nothing global. It is the self-serve expression of item 1: no credit card, straight into onboarding. Next: 2.1 Log in (return access), then the 2.2 Magic-link sent and 2.3 Invalid or expired link states.
