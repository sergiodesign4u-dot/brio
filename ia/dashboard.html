Dashboard

The operator's home and the pulse aha, 1 tap from login. In one screen: a plain-language read of how the team is doing, what it means, and the one thing to do next (the interpretive layer, E1). This is where D1 pays off, the aggregate appears only at minimum-N, with no live count. Serves E1, J1, and J2. Inside the operator app, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/dashboard.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up.

  1. Nav + greeting (0.1 operator, Dashboard active): a light greeting, no jargon.
  2. Pulse card (the aha): the interpretive layer in one place, a plain-language score (a word, not a bare number), a one-line interpretation, and one suggested next action. The recurring main-job value.
  3. Trend spark + participation: a small sparkline and the participation rate as a share ("72% answered"), never who.
  4. Threshold-alert banner (DX1, contextual): appears only on a meaningful dip; plain-language, deep-links to 4.2; also the alert-notification landing.
  5. Privacy-proof mini (DX2): "Aggregate only. You never see who answered," linking to 1.1. Condensed reuse of 0.3.
  6. Quick links: 4.1 full pulse, Programs (5.0), Share with owner (7.0) on a positive milestone.
  7. App shell footer (minimal): privacy link (1.1) and help.

Components used: 0.1 Navigation, 0.3 Privacy-disclosure (condensed, DX2). The pulse card, trend spark, and threshold-alert banner are thin in-node dashboard elements, not new globals. No new global invented.

E1, feel competent

The interpretive layer

E1 is a core MVP job: make the non-expert operator feel competent. So the pulse never shows a bare metric. Every state of the pulse card carries three parts, so the operator always knows what happened, what it means, and what to do, with no HR training.

PartExample
1. Plain-language reading"Steady"
2. What it means (one line)"The team is holding steady this week, in line with last."
3. One suggested next action"Share this with your owner" (to 7.0)

Private app zone

SEO block (noindex)

Dashboard is inside the operator app, an authenticated per-account view. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The in-app findability rules still apply.

robotsnoindex, nofollow
canonicalnone for search; app URL is /app
schema.orgnone (not a public landing page)
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the greeting or "Your team pulse") for the accessible document outline
  • the pulse score and interpretation are text, not color-only; a screen reader hears the reading, not just a color
  • the trend spark has a text equivalent (the participation share and direction)
  • tap targets at least 44px
The aha, reuse only. Dashboard reuses 0.1 and 0.3 (condensed) and invents nothing global; the pulse card, trend spark, and alert banner are thin in-node elements. It hosts the registered states 4.3 and 4.4 where D1 becomes visible. Next: 4.1 Team pulse detail (the full read behind the card), then 4.2 Threshold alert detail.
