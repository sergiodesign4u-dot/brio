Security and privacy

The public, indexable instance of the canonical 0.3 privacy-disclosure: Brio's differentiator made public, a fuller, third-person, crawlable version of the same five claims. It explains how the aggregate-only model works so a prospective operator (and any employee who looks it up) can trust the model before signing up. This is the only crawlable version of the five claims, so its copy is the canonical public source and must not drift from the spine. Serves the Main job, the benchmark differentiator, and J3 and E2. Indexed, so full A to E SEO. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/security-privacy.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

The page's job is trust, so the strongest element is the plain, honest explanation, not the CTA. It says the same thing the team sees before answering (10.1) and the operator sees in the Privacy center (8.2).

  1. Public nav (0.1 pre-auth): the Brio mark, marketing links (Pricing, Security active, About), Log in, Start free. No job tabs.
  2. Hero: H1 "Your team's answers stay private", a subhead, and Start free. The LCP is the heading text.
  3. How the aggregate-only model works: a plain three-step explanation (your answer joins an aggregate, results appear only above a minimum of 5, the owner sees a team number, never an individual).
  4. The five claims (public framing): the canonical 0.3 spine, fuller and third-person.
  5. What the owner can and cannot see: the can and cannot panel (the same wf-see and wf-seerow used at 7.0, 8.2, 9.0).
  6. Privacy questions (FAQ): real questions and answers, marked up as an FAQ (the FAQPage schema source).
  7. CTA band and legal link: Start free, to 2.0 Sign up, plus a link to the full Privacy Policy (1.6). Footer (0.2).

Components used: 0.1 Navigation (public), 0.2 Footer, the canonical 0.3 privacy-disclosure (public / explainer variant, the five claims and the can and cannot panel), and the public chrome from 1.0. No new global; 1.1 is the public, indexable 0.3 instance (operator, owner, and employee instances are noindex), so this page carries the crawlable copy and must not drift.

C. Ready SEO text

Brio is built so that no one, not a manager and not the owner, ever sees an individual employee's answer. Every response joins a team aggregate, and no score is shown until at least five people have answered, so a single reply cannot be picked out. While a check-in is open, no one sees a running count. The owner sees a team wellbeing score, its trend, and how many people took part, never who said what. This is the product, not a setting: the subscription pays for the aggregate, and individual data is never shown or sold.
