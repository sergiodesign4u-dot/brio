Submit error

The failure-at-submit case: the 10.2 submit did not go through (a connection drop or a server error), so the answers are kept and it is recovered with a one-tap Try again, never a dead-end. It is the employee-side twin of the operator "could not save, the form stays filled" rule at 5.2 and 3.3. For Yemi the employee. Serves J4 and the F2 no-dead-end rule. Closes cluster 10. Private token-gated surface, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/checkin-submit-error.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Even the failure keeps the calm near-anonymous surface, with the anonymity anchor present. The whole point is the answers-saved reassurance: the employee never has to answer twice.

  1. Minimal top bar with the anonymity anchor: the Brio mark, the company name, and the "Anonymous" reminder.
  2. Attention mark: a dashed badge with an "!" (not a check), so the error reads without relying on color (the 10.4 treatment).
  3. Plain, non-blaming explanation: "We could not send your answers", with a one-line honest reason (a connection problem, or something on our side).
  4. The key reassurance, answers preserved: a clear line and a small "answers saved" indicator that the entered answers are held on this screen and nothing was lost.
  5. Try again: the primary action, which re-submits the saved answers (back into 10.2, no re-entry). A quiet "still not working? give it a moment" fallback.

Components used: the 10.0 check-in chrome (no nav, no account), the 10.4 attention treatment (dashed "!" badge), and the 10.4 reassurance block re-pointed to the answers-saved message. No new global; no personal data appears, only the anchor, the reassurance, and the small answers-saved indicator.

Primary CTA: Try again, which re-submits the saved answers (to 10.2, then 10.3 on success). The answers remain preserved either way. No account step, no other navigation, and never a request to answer twice, by design.

Private token-gated surface

SEO block (noindex)

Submit error is a private, token-gated employee surface. It is not indexed and carries no schema. It is a transactional zone, per the platform SEO stance.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (token-gated, personal)
canonicalnone for search; flow URL is /c/{invite-token}
schema.orgnone
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the explanation heading) for the accessible document outline
  • the error is conveyed by text and the "!" mark, never by color alone; the answers-saved reassurance is text, not color
  • Try again is a labelled control; the "answers saved" indicator is legible text
  • tap targets at least 44px; the flow works without an account
A failure, not a wall, and no answering twice. Submit error is the employee-side version of the "could not save, the form stays filled" rule from 5.2 and 3.3: a failed submit becomes a plain explanation, an answers-saved reassurance, and a one-tap Try again. It reuses the 10.0 chrome, the 10.4 attention treatment, and the 10.4 reassurance block, and invents nothing global. This closes cluster 10 and the whole employee check-in surface.
