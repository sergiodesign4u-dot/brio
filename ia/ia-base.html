Information Architecture

Base Layer: the structure of Brio

The concept layer of the IA: what screens exist, how they group, and how a person moves between them. Every screen serves a job from the JTBD. A screen with no job is an orphan; a job with no screen is a hole. The traceability matrix catches both. Structure is derived from jobs, not copied from a competitor menu.

Focus

Priya, the Handed-It Operator (primary persona) x her main job. Three surfaces: the operator app (primary), the owner view (secondary, aggregate-only), the employee check-in (third-party, near-anonymous).

"When I am handed responsibility for employee wellbeing with no HR training, I want a guided system that tells me what to run and when, so that I feel competent and the team actually benefits."

Source: user-research/docs/personas.md, jtbd.md, cjm-to-be.md (traced backlog). Detail: ia/docs/sitemap.md, flows.md.

No orphan in the tree: every node cites a job. Two nodes are supporting infrastructure (Log in, Account), they gate the operator's jobs rather than close their own, kept and flagged. Parked (deliberately not nodes): employee "see team result" (HJ2 [?]), referral / share-to-network (S1, E3 [?]), custom nudge scheduling (LATER, paid). Not invented (competitor menu, no job): leaderboards, drill-down analytics, custom program builder (Stage 2 cut list).
Routes

User Flows

The main job plus three related jobs, each proving the route: steps, decision diamonds, states, and both ends, success and dead-end. Color is semantic.

Success: happy-path ends and the arrows that reach them Dead-end: no path to the goal Neutral: steps, decisions, loading / empty, recoverable error
Step 6 critique applied. Dead-end exits added (sub-N explainer, employee "see what your manager sees" detour, recoverable free-tier exit, digest re-surface of an ignored alert) and missing states added (already-submitted, owner "score not ready", invite-send error).

Navigation Model

Global navigation is the shortest path for the primary persona to her main job, not "what to put in a menu." Each global item is an entry into a job-cluster. The owner and employee surfaces have no multi-tab nav by design.

Global navigation (operator app)

Global itemJob-cluster behind itWhy global
Dashboard (home)E1 + J1 + J2: the pulse signal (score + interpretation + next action)The recurring aha and the reason she logs in. Post-login home
ProgramsMain + J6: run wellbeing (library, pick, manage)The core "what do I run" job, entered repeatedly
TeamMain + item 3: invite + participation (aggregate)Entered on every new hire, the invite / roster surface
OwnerJ1 + J5 + S2 + item 8: prove it works + upgradeTop-3 operator job by importance and the paid-conversion path
Settings utilitysupporting + item 4: Plan, Privacy center, Notifications, AccountUtility cluster, corner entry, not a job tab

Trade-off: "Owner" is only ~monthly-frequency and could be a contextual "Share this result" action instead. It is kept global because the owner dashboard is the paid feature and J1 / J5 / S2 score 3 for Priya. Reversible if usage shows it is rarely a fresh entry.

One intentional exception, not a violation: the first-run guided onboarding is a linear wizard (company setup, first program confirm, invite team). This is sequential flow depth, not navigational depth. It is J6 itself ("tells me exactly what to do and in what order"), budgeted by time (under 5 minutes), runs once, and is not reachable from global nav afterward.

Global / contextual / deep

Global (always visible): the 4 job tabs (Dashboard, Programs, Team, Owner) plus Settings utility. Owner surface = single dashboard; employee surface = no nav.

Contextual (inside a flow): "Share this result with your owner" (on a positive milestone), "Set up check-in" (inside a program), "Resend invite" (inside roster), threshold-alert banner (on the Dashboard when it fires), pre-Q1 privacy disclosure (inside the check-in), upgrade prompt (on a free-tier limit).

Deep (rare): Plan / subscription change, Privacy center detail, Notification cadence, Account, and the one-time onboarding wizard.

Step 6 depth fixes

DX1
Threshold alert reach. Reached in 1 tap as a contextual Dashboard banner and a notification deep-link, not only 3-deep via nav. The urgent early-warning job (J2) is not buried.
DX2
Privacy proof visibility. The operator-facing privacy proof is not only 2 taps deep in Settings, a summary is also surfaced at the onboarding confirmation and on the Dashboard, so item 4 ("visible to the operator as proof") is met without a deep hunt.
