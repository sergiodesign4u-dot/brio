About

The supporting brand page: who Brio is and why it exists, so a prospective operator trusts the company, not just the features. The mission (wellbeing without surveillance), the values that map to the design principles, the team, and a way to get in touch. Indexed, with a full A to E SEO block and the Organization schema. Team bios and photos are a hypothesis pending. For Priya the operator (pre-auth). Serves supporting brand trust. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/about.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

The page's job is trust and brand, so the strongest element is the mission, not the CTA. It reinforces the differentiator as a company belief, not a tactic (Principle 1 and Principle 4).

  1. Public nav (0.1 pre-auth): the Brio mark, marketing links (Pricing, Security, About active), Log in, Start free. No job tabs.
  2. Hero: H1 "Wellbeing without surveillance", a subhead, and Start free. The LCP is the heading text.
  3. Our mission: why Brio exists (people work handed out with no HR; wellbeing should never mean watching people; the privacy boundary is the belief).
  4. What we believe: a few values that map to the design principles.
  5. The team: a small grid of people (name and role), placeholders; real bios and photos are [?] pending.
  6. Get in touch: a link to 1.3 Contact. CTA band: Start free, to 2.0. Footer (0.2).

Components used: 0.1 Navigation (public), 0.2 Footer, and the public chrome from 1.0 and 1.1. The mission, values, and team grid are page-specific marketing layout. No new global; team bios and photos are a hypothesis, and compliance badges are deliberately not shown pending legal review.

C. Ready SEO text

Brio exists because small companies get handed people work with no HR and no playbook, and because wellbeing should never mean watching people. We build employee wellbeing software that is guided enough for a non-expert to run and private by design: individual answers are never shown to anyone, because the privacy boundary is the product, not a setting. We would rather show an owner an honest team signal than a flattering vanity metric.
