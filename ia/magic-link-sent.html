Magic-link sent

A "check your email" confirmation that holds the operator calmly while they go to their inbox. A small, honest waiting state: reassure, show where the link went so a typo is caught, and offer a low-friction resend. The forward action lives in the email. One state serves three paths: the magic-link option on Sign up and Log in, and the forgot-password reset. Transient and transactional, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/magic-link-sent.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up. This is a waiting state, so the on-screen job is deliberately small.

  1. Pre-auth header: the Brio mark and a "Back to log in" link (a reduced 0.1 variant, no job tabs).
  2. Confirmation mark and heading: a simple black-and-white confirmation badge and "Check your email". Calm, done, not alarming.
  3. Body with the target address: "We sent a link to you@company.com. Open it to continue." Showing the address lets the operator catch a typo.
  4. Open-mail hint: a helper to open the mail app, plus the link's expiry ("expires in about 15 minutes"), so expectations are set before it lapses to 2.3.
  5. Resend: "Didn't get it? Resend" with a short cooldown, and "Use a different email" back to 2.0 or 2.1.

Components used: a reduced 0.1 (pre-auth header), the auth-page shell from 2.0, and a simple confirmation badge. No new global. Adaptive copy: the same structure serves the reset path; only the verb changes ("log in" versus "reset your password").

Waiting, resend, and outcome

States and primary action

There is no forward button by design: the primary action is the link in the email. The screen only reassures and helps recover a lost email.

Transient transactional state

SEO block (noindex, nofollow)

Magic-link sent is a transient, transactional state reached only after a request. It is not indexed and has nothing to crawl. Unlike the 2.0 and 2.1 forms (which keep follow for outbound equity), this is a dead-end waiting state.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (transient state, no meaningful outbound links)
reached bya POST from 2.0 / 2.1, not a browse target; not linked from marketing pages

In-app findability (still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the heading) for the accessible document outline
  • the target email address is selectable text
  • the expiry and the resend cooldown are stated in text, not implied by color
  • Resend and Use a different email are labelled controls; tap targets at least 44px
One state, three paths. Magic-link sent reuses the auth-page shell from 2.0 and a reduced 0.1; it invents nothing global, and the single state serves the sign-up magic link, the log-in magic link, and the password reset. It is deliberately a small, honest waiting screen: the forward action is the email link, and an expired link routes cleanly to 2.3.
