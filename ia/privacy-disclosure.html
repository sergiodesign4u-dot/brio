Privacy-disclosure component

The differentiator made into UI. In an SMB there is no HR layer between the boss and personal data, so the privacy boundary is shown in the product, not promised in a policy. One canonical set of claims, rendered in role variants on the employee, operator, owner, and public surfaces. It is the trust gate for honest participation (H2, still [?]). No H1. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/privacy-disclosure.md.

One promise, everywhere

Canonical privacy spine

The same five claims appear in every instance. The wording is [?] pending final UX and legal copy; the claims are fixed.

  • Aggregate only. No named responses, ever. No individual timeline, no drill-down.
  • Minimum-N of 5. No score until at least 5 people respond (indirect-identification protection, benchmarked to Culture Amp).
  • No live count during an open cycle. No running tally for operator or employee while a cycle is open, closing the small-team timing-inference vector (founder decision D1).
  • Subscription as privacy. The owner pays for aggregate insight; the model is aligned with never exposing individual data, stated plainly.
  • What the owner can and cannot see, listed explicitly.

Source: research/strategy.md Section 5 (D1, no live count), research/docs/benchmark.md (minimum-N, indirect identification), user-research/docs/cjm-to-be.md (privacy bundle), CLAUDE.md privacy mechanism.

Structure SEO and a11y

SEO and accessibility

  • No H1; it is a component, not a page.
  • On the employee, operator, and owner surfaces it is content in noindex zones, so not an SEO surface there. The public 1.1 Security and privacy page renders the indexable version of the same content; the two must not drift.
  • The expander is a disclosure widget: a <button aria-expanded> toggling a labelled region.
  • The can / cannot panel uses semantic lists; the whole component is keyboard reachable and screen-reader legible.
Canonical reuse. Defined once here. Rendered by 10.1 (employee), 8.2 + 3.1 + 4.0 (operator), 9.1 (owner), 1.1 (public). Change the spine here, propagate to all five. Single source of the privacy-promise content. Next global component: 0.4 Program card.
