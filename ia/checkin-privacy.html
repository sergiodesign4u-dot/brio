Pre-Q1 privacy disclosure

Before the employee answers anything, show, not just tell, that their individual response is never seen. This renders the canonical 0.3 privacy-disclosure component in its employee variant: five plain claims plus a "see exactly what your manager sees" expander that opens the real, aggregate-only owner view. It is persistent, shown before Question 1 every cycle. This is the trust-critical screen where the riskiest assumption (H2, honest participation at or above 65%) is won or lost. Serves J3, E2, item 4; on flow F2. Private token-gated surface, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/checkin-privacy.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block order reasoned from the 360px baseline up. The disclosure is the only thing between entry (10.0) and Question 1 (10.2); it must be read, not skipped past chrome. This is Principle 3 (earn trust before asking for data) and Principle 1 (privacy is the product) made into a screen.

  1. Minimal check-in top bar: the Brio mark and the company name ("for Acme"), no navigation and no account controls (the 10.0 chrome carried through).
  2. Section heading: a plain-language title ("Before you answer"), the screen H1 for the document outline. The 0.3 component itself carries no H1, but the screen does.
  3. The five claims: each an icon plus one plain sentence, in the employee variant, stacked on a phone. The body of the disclosure.
  4. "See exactly what your manager sees" expander: an employee-only disclosure that opens an aggregate-only mock (the exact owner view: a team number, a trend, a participation share, and a "cannot see" line). The promise shown, not just told. F2 DE2 recovery for the skeptical employee.
  5. Continue: the primary action, to 10.2 Questions.
  6. Leave: a quiet exit with no penalty; nothing has been asked or recorded yet.

Components used: the canonical 0.3 privacy-disclosure (employee variant) on the 10.0 check-in chrome. The expander is a disclosure widget (a button aria-expanded toggling a labelled region). The aggregate-only mock reuses the 9.0 / 4.0 pulse-read element, so the employee sees the same aggregate an owner would, never an individual. No new global is invented; this is a role render of an existing one.

Canonical spine, employee variant

The five claims

This section is one render of the 0.3 component. The five claims are fixed; the wording is [?] pending final UX and legal copy. The employee variant leads with "no one sees your individual response" and frames each claim as "your answer".

ClaimEmployee framingBasis
1. Aggregate onlyNo one sees your individual response. No named list, no individual timeline.Differentiator, Principle 1
2. Minimum-N of 5Your answer joins an aggregate of at least 5, so no single response can be picked out.Culture Amp indirect-ID protection
3. No live countNo running count while the check-in is open, so no one can guess who answered when.Founder decision D1
4. Subscription as privacyThe owner pays for the team view, never for your answers.Business model aligned to privacy
5. What the owner seesResults appear only after the cycle closes, and only as a team number, trend, and participation share.Item 4, E2
Below minimum-N. When the team is under 5, claim 5 is replaced by "Results need at least 5 people. If fewer answer this cycle, no results are shown at all." No live count is still enforced. The same honest limit as 4.3 and 9.3, surfaced not hidden, which is what makes it credible on a small team ("my dept only has 3 of us").

Private token-gated surface

SEO block (noindex)

Pre-Q1 disclosure is a private, token-gated employee surface. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The indexable version of the same five canonical claims lives on the public 1.1 Security and privacy page; the two must not drift (0.3 is the single source).

robotsnoindex, nofollow (token-gated, personal)
canonicalnone for search; flow URL is /c/{invite-token}
schema.orgnone
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the section heading "Before you answer") for the accessible document outline
  • each claim is text with an icon, never carried by color alone; the below-N swap uses a dashed mark plus words, not color
  • the expander is a button aria-expanded toggling a labelled region; the aggregate mock is legible to a screen reader as text
  • Continue and Leave are labelled controls; tap targets at least 44px; the flow works without an account
The trust gate. Pre-Q1 disclosure is the differentiator (aggregate-only privacy) rendered as the last thing the employee reads before answering, with the "see exactly what your manager sees" expander turning the promise into something shown, not just told. It renders the canonical 0.3 component (employee variant) and reuses the 9.0 pulse-read mock; it invents nothing global. This is where H2 (honest participation at or above 65%) is won or lost.
