IA / Detail Layer / Structure / Footer (0.2)

Footer

The second global structural node and the second internal-linking plane after Navigation. Full on public and marketing pages, reduced in the operator app, absent on the owner and employee surfaces. It closes no job of its own; it repeats the privacy trust signal on every page and carries the crawlable SEO and legal links. No H1. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/footer.md.

Structure

Anatomy

Top to bottom, four parts. Each link is a real destination, registered as a node so the map and the footer cannot drift.

  • Trust strip (above the footer, every page): one line, "Aggregate-only wellbeing. We never show individual responses." The privacy promise restated in the UI, not in a policy (the benchmark differentiator).
  • Columns: Product (How it works, Pricing 1.0, Security and privacy 1.1), Company (About 1.2, Contact 1.3), Resources (Help and FAQ 1.4, Programs overview), Legal (Privacy Policy, Terms, Cookie Policy 1.6, Cookie settings 11.3).
  • SEO popular-links block: crawlable links to priority pages, the second linking plane. Structure fixed; exact list [?] pending keyword research. Points at the 1.5 landing template.
  • Bottom row: copyright, cookie settings, social (LinkedIn [?]). No compliance badges (SOC 2, GDPR): [?] pending legal review, not claimed before it.

Structure SEO and a11y

SEO and accessibility

  • No H1; semantic <footer> (contentinfo landmark).
  • Second internal-linking plane: all links are real crawlable <a>, not script-driven.
  • Mobile accordions render collapsed but stay in the initial DOM, so their links remain crawlable. Google indexes content hidden in collapsed accordions and tabs when it is in the server-rendered HTML, not injected after load.
  • The SEO popular-links block is structure only; final copy and real query volumes are deferred to production ([?]).
  • Cookie settings re-opens the consent UI (11.3); the legal pages (1.6) are indexable.

Sources: Google indexing of collapsed and tabbed content (Illyes 2016; Mueller 2020), summarized at Search Engine Journal and Oneupweb.

Canonical reuse. Defined once here and referenced by every public and marketing node. The trust strip is the same one line everywhere; the legal set (1.6) is one shared template rendering three documents. Change the footer here, propagate to all copies.
