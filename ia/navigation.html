Navigation: header and mobile tabs

The first structural node and the exemplar for the rest. A global shell inherited on every operator-app page, with a reduced variant on the owner and employee surfaces. It closes no job of its own; it gates every operator job and is the shortest path for Priya to her main job. As a global component it carries no H1. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/navigation.md.

Grounded, not by eye

Pattern basis

The tab set is chosen from established mobile-navigation research, not picked by feel. Five findings shape it:

  • 3 to 5 primary destinations in a bottom bar; more than 5 crowds tap targets and hurts accuracy. Brio uses 4 job tabs; Settings is a utility, not a fifth tab.
  • Tap target at least 44px (WCAG 2.2 Enhanced, AAA; Apple 44pt; Material 3 48dp). Brio mobile tabs use a 48px minimum with spacing.
  • Icon plus text label, never icon alone.
  • Fixed bottom bar, all options always visible, no reflow.
  • A bottom bar is for primary destinations of equal importance switched frequently, not for secondary or hierarchical actions. Settings and account stay out of the primary bar.

Sources: NN/g, Basic Patterns for Mobile Navigation · Smashing, Golden Rules of Mobile Navigation Design · WCAG 2.2 Target Size (Enhanced) · Apple HIG, Tab Bars · Material 3, Navigation bar

Structure SEO and a11y

SEO and accessibility

  • The global component carries no H1; the H1 belongs to each page.
  • Semantic <nav aria-label="Primary">, preceded by a skip-to-content link for keyboard users.
  • aria-current="page" on the active destination; the active state is also visible (filled icon plus label plus indicator), not color-only.
  • Tap targets at least 44px, 48px on the mobile bottom bar, with spacing to prevent mis-taps.
  • Icon is always paired with a visible text label.
  • The guest and marketing nav (Landing, Pricing) is a crawlable internal-linking surface. The authenticated app nav sits in noindex zones, so there it is wayfinding, not an SEO surface.
  • The employee and owner variants are reduced (no tabs); the employee top bar carries the persistent privacy assurance line, which is content, not navigation.
