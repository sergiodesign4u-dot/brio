Share with owner

The operator's share moment. Give the owner a high-level, aggregate-only read of team wellbeing, and make the privacy boundary unmistakable before anyone is invited: the operator sees exactly what the owner will and will not see, then invites. Because the owner dashboard is a paid feature, the invite gates on the plan (paid invites directly, free routes to the upgrade). Serves J1, J5, S2; fires from a positive milestone on the Dashboard (F3). Inside the operator app, so noindex. Source of truth: ia/docs/pages/share-owner.md.

Mobile-first priority

Content blocks

Block 1 is the first screen on mobile. Order reasoned from the 360px baseline up. The privacy boundary appears before any invite, never after.

  1. 0.1 Navigation: the operator app nav with the Owner tab active.
  2. Heading and context: "Give your owner the big picture", plus a contextual line when reached from a positive milestone.
  3. What your owner will see (aggregate-only preview): the exact owner view, team score, trend, and participation as a share, reusing the pulse element from 4.0 / 4.1.
  4. The privacy boundary (sees / never sees): the 0.3 owner variant as two plain lists. Sees the aggregate; never sees individual responses, names, who answered, or any drill-down.
  5. Invite your owner (primary, with the plan gate): paid proceeds to invite (owner email) toward 9.0; free routes to 7.1 for the paid unlock. The paid nature is stated honestly here.

Components used: 0.1 (Owner tab active), 0.3 owner-facing variant (the sees / never-sees boundary), and the pulse preview from 4.0 / 4.1. No new global; the boundary is a 0.3 variant.

Operator app zone

SEO block (noindex)

Share with owner is inside the authenticated operator app. It is not indexed and carries no schema. The in-app findability rules still apply.

robotsnoindex, nofollow (deep app zone, like Dashboard and Team)
canonicalnone for search; app URL is /app/owner
schema.orgnone
hreflang / OGnone

In-app findability (not SEO, still specified)

  • exactly one H1 (the heading) for the accessible document outline
  • the sees / never-sees boundary is conveyed in text and labels, not by color alone
  • the preview values are selectable text; participation is stated as a share, never as who
  • Invite your owner is a labelled primary control that states when it leads to an upgrade; tap targets at least 44px
Trust shown, not promised. Share with owner reuses 0.1, the 0.3 owner-facing privacy variant, and the pulse preview from 4.0 / 4.1; it invents nothing global. It is the paid-visibility moment of flow F3, and it makes the aggregate-only promise visible in the product exactly where trust is spent: before the owner is invited, not in a policy afterward. Next: 7.1 Invite owner + upgrade (the owner email and the paid unlock), then the 7.2 Upgrade dialog and the 7.3 Payment failed state.
