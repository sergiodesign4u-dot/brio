# Product Model - v1 (pre-research hypotheses)

*Label: v1 - pre-research hypotheses. To be validated in Phases 3 and 4.*

---

## 1. Product Objectives

| # | Objective | Metric | Target (hypothesis) |
|---|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| O1 | Operator feels competent running wellbeing without HR training | % of operators who complete setup and launch first program within 7 days of signup | 70% |
| O2 | Owner sees credible team-level signal | % of owner sessions where they view the aggregate dashboard and do not bounce within 30 seconds | 60% |
| O3 | Employee trust in privacy model drives participation | Team participation rate in check-ins (% of invited employees who respond) | 65% average across active accounts |
| O4 | Product delivers measurable business retention | Monthly churn rate for accounts past 90-day mark | Below 4% |

## 2. Audience Segments

### Segment A - The Operator (Primary)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Age | 26 to 45 |
| Profile | Office manager, ops coordinator, EA, or founder at a 10-200 person company with no dedicated HR. Often women. Organized, empathetic, over-extended. Has been handed "people stuff" alongside their actual job. |
| Motivation | Do right by the team, feel competent, not get blamed when people are unhappy |
| Pain | No training or framework. No HR to ask. Guessing. Spends time on ad hoc gestures (birthday cakes, team lunches) without knowing if they help. Anxious about employee attrition. |
| JTBD | "When I am responsible for employee wellbeing with no HR background, I want a guided system that tells me what to do and when, so that I feel confident and the team actually benefits." |
| Priority | Primary |
| Reason | Daily driver. Without operator engagement, there is no product. Their competence and confidence directly determines whether employees see value. |

### Segment B - The Owner (Secondary)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Age | 35 to 55 |
| Profile | Founder, CEO, or managing partner of a 10-200 person company. Business-minded, time-poor, cares about the team but not a "people person." Approves budget. |
| Motivation | Team retention, productivity, proof that spend is justified |
| Pain | No visibility into team health without asking directly (which skews the answer). Worried about losing key people. Does not want to manage wellbeing but does not want to ignore it. |
| JTBD | "When I need to know if my team investment is working, I want a clear, honest team-level signal without having to read through individual responses, so that I can make confident decisions about people and budget." |
| Priority | Secondary |
| Reason | Buyer and approver. Sees the outcome dashboard. Their conviction that the product works determines renewal. Not a daily driver. |

### Segment C - The Employee (Third party - privacy hard constraint)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Age | 22 to 55 |
| Profile | Any employee at the company. Diverse roles and backgrounds. Skeptical of employer-run wellbeing tools ("is this surveillance?"). Appreciates feeling heard but only if it is genuinely private. |
| Motivation | Feel supported, not monitored. Participate if it feels safe. Benefit from programs that are actually useful. |
| Pain | Fear that their responses will identify them to the boss. Distrust of corporate wellness theater. Cynicism about tools that claim to care but feel like data collection. |
| JTBD | "When I am asked how I am doing at work, I want to know my answer is private and actually influences something, so that I bother to respond honestly." |
| Priority | Third party (not buyer, not operator) |
| Reason | Their trust and participation rate is the data quality engine. Without honest employee responses, operator and owner signals are meaningless. Their individual data is a hard privacy constraint - never surfaced up the chain. |

## 3. AIDA per Segment

### Segment A - Operator

| Stage | Channel | Message | Mechanic |
|-------|---------|---------|----------|
| Awareness | LinkedIn ads, content marketing (blog, SEO), word of mouth from founder/owner | "You got handed HR. Here is a system that actually works." | Pain-point headline targeting "office manager" and "ops" search intent |
| Interest | Landing page, case study content, free trial CTA | "Set up your first wellbeing program in under 5 minutes - no HR degree required." | Interactive demo or product tour, before/after scenario |
| Desire | Free trial activation, in-app onboarding | The product itself shows them what to do next with zero ambiguity | Guided setup flow, first program live within session, immediate positive feedback loop |
| Action | First program launched, team invited | "Your team's first check-in is ready. Share this link." | One-click team invite, email template pre-written |

### Segment B - Owner

| Stage | Channel | Message | Mechanic |
|-------|---------|---------|----------|
| Awareness | Operator referral, LinkedIn, podcast sponsorship | "Know if your team is OK without having to ask." | Aggregate team signal framing, ROI angle |
| Interest | Landing page owner section, operator sharing dashboard screenshot | "One honest number. Team health at a glance." | Show the owner dashboard clearly, emphasize what they do NOT see (individual data) |
| Desire | Operator activates free trial, owner receives invite to view dashboard | Seeing a real (even if placeholder) aggregate score and trend makes it concrete | Owner-specific onboarding: 2-minute explainer of what they can see and cannot see |
| Action | Owner approves paid upgrade, becomes internal sponsor | "The team is at 73/100 this month, up 8 points. Here is what the operator ran." | Monthly digest email to owner, one-click upgrade prompt |

### Segment C - Employee

| Stage | Channel | Message | Mechanic |
|-------|---------|---------|----------|
| Awareness | Internal invite from operator (email or Slack) | "We are trying something new for team wellbeing. Takes 30 seconds." | Invite from a trusted colleague (the operator), not a cold tool |
| Interest | First check-in experience | "Your response is anonymous - [company name] only sees the team score, not your answer." | Explicit privacy statement at first check-in, no login required option |
| Desire | Program content (challenges, resources) | "This week's challenge: 10 minutes outside. Join 7 of your colleagues." | Social proof without names ("7 colleagues"), opt-in gentle nudge |
| Action | Ongoing participation | Habit formed, routine check-in | Low friction (under 30 seconds), consistent schedule, visible aggregate outcome ("the team hit 80% this month") |

## 4. AIDA to AARRR Mapping

| AIDA Stage | AARRR Stage | Rationale |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| Awareness | Acquisition | Top-of-funnel discovery: ads, SEO, word of mouth |
| Interest | Acquisition + Activation | Landing page and first trial session overlap both stages |
| Desire | Activation | The product experience creates conviction during trial |
| Action (first program) | Activation | First meaningful product action = activated |
| Repeat use, team engagement | Retention | Ongoing operator habit, employee participation |
| Upgrade, plan expansion | Revenue | Conversion from free to paid, seat expansion |
| Referral, case study | Referral | Operator recommends to peer, owner talks at a roundtable |
